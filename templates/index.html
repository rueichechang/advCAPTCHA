CONSENT DOCUMENT FOR ENROLLING PARTICIPANTS IN A RESEARCH STUDY

 

Georgia Institute of Technology

 

Exploring Cognitively Lighter Audio CAPTCHAs

 

Investigators: Valerie Fanelle, Sepideh Karimi, Aditi Shah, and Bharath Subramanian

 

You are being asked to be a volunteer in a research study. You are encouraged to take your time in making your decision regarding your participation in this study.

 

Purpose:

The purpose of this study is to contrast the usability and security of current audio CAPTCHAs to a series of new audio CAPTCHA designs.

 

Exclusion/Inclusion Criteria:

Participants must be above 18 years of age, and have legally blind vision.

 

Procedures:

If you decide to be in this study, your part will involve one online interview session lasting no more than 45 minutes. Remember, you are free to leave at any time throughout the duration of the study. Upon initiation, you will first be presented with a link to a web-platform containing a series of audio clips and questions. As you listen to these clips and complete the survey questions, we ask for you to communicate to us your reactions and feelings towards each design. Upon completion, you will navigate to a given page that presents you with a demographic survey. Your individual demographic information, time to complete each question, and question scores will be recorded and kept separated from personally identifying information. Your data will be coded by your participant number, not your name. After completing the demographic survey, you will be debriefed by one of the researchers, explaining the procedure and importance of the study in more detail and answering any further questions you might have.

 

Risks or Discomforts:

The risks involved are no greater than those involved in daily activities such as normal electronic device usage, similar to logging into a website.

 

Benefits:

You are not likely to benefit in any tangible way from joining this study, apart from research enhancing the possibility of more comfortable and user friendly CAPTCHA designs.

 

Compensation to You:

There will be a compensation for participation in this study of 10$ (USD).

 

Storing and Sharing your Information:

Your participation in this study is gratefully acknowledged.  It is possible that your

information/data will be enormously valuable for other research purposes.  By signing below, you consent for your de-identified information/data to be stored by the researcher and to be shared with other researchers in future studies.  If you agree to allow such future sharing and use, your identity will be completely separated from your information/data.  Future researchers will not have a way to identify you.  Any future research must be approved by an ethics committee before being undertaken.

 

Confidentiality:

The following procedures will be followed to keep your personal information confidential in this study:  Your privacy will be protected to the extent required by law.  To protect your privacy, your records will be kept under a participant number rather than by name.  Your records will be kept in locked files and unless you give specific consent otherwise, only members of this research group will be allowed to look at them.  Your name and any other fact that might point to you will not appear when results of this study are presented or published. The Georgia Institute of Technology IRB, the Office of Human Research Protections, and/or the Food and Drug Administration may look over study records during required reviews.

 

You should be aware that the experiment is not being run from a ‘secure’ https server of the kind typically used to handle credit card transactions, so there is a small possibility that responses could be viewed by unauthorized third parties such as computer hackers.  In general, the web page software will log as header lines the IP address of the machine you use to access this page, e.g.,102.403.506.807, but otherwise no other information will be stored unless you explicitly enter it.

 

Costs to You:

There are no costs to you, other than your time, for being in this study.
